The Personal Computer




The personal computer in some sense made everyone feel like they were part of the action in Silicon Valley. Prior to Apple Computer and Microsoft's early computing devices, the computer was seen as a bulky, inaccessible, expensive object used only by scientists and professionals.23 This was largely due to problems in hardware; without reliable networks of transistors it was very difficult to fit sufficient quantities of computational power into a small space. After the introduction of the PC, however, the computer slowly became a household object, and over the past 30 years no technology has become quite as "prolific."

Moreover, the computer had a large effect on Silicon Valley itself. With PCs and later laptops, individuals were not confined to the office when working on projects. As a result, technological development was now a 24-hour nonstop marathon. This meant more productivity, more efficiency, and, as often seems to be the goal, more money.24 This continued to accentuate the gap between rich and poor, adversely affecting living conditions for unemployed or non-tech-savvy Silicon Valley residents.25
