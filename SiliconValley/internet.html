Social Media




By far the most important part of Silicon Valley today is the social media industry. Facebook, Twitter, Snapchat, Yahoo!, and dozens of other internet-based social platforms are headquartered in the region. Moreover, any aspiring programmer who hopes to one day be the next Mark Zuckerberg (Facebook) or Biz Stone (Twitter) feels obligated to relocate to Silicon Valley. 26 This comes as no surprise considering the reach that most major media platforms have: Facebook has over 1.3 billion users.27

Even with these massive successes, it still seems like social media is just getting started. Facebook, for example, is only ten years old. Who knows what another ten years of innovation will bring to the field? Artificial intelligence (AI) research is certainly at the forefront at the moment too. There's no reason to believe that, just like with gold in 1849 and the transistor in 1949, new developments in social media won't cause another boom in, say, 2049. And if/when they do, there's no doubt Silicon Valley will be the epicenter of it all.
